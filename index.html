Slime Simulation

Overview

This interactive simulation uses NEAT (NeuroEvolution of Augmenting Topologies) to train slimes to hunt for food (green triangles). Each slime’s color and size represents its species, determined by its neural network connections. Each slime’s neural network decides its movement based on the position of the closest food, choosing to move up, down, left, or right. Slime movement toward food is not pre-programmed, each slime learns the best way to reach its food through evolution.

Controls

  • Press Start to begin the simulation.
  • If the simulation is already running, pressing Start again will completely reset and restart it.
  • Click on a slime to pause the simulation and view its neural network.

Evolution & Rounds

At the start of the simulation, every slime is mutated three times to encourage early progress. After each round, the top three species that collect the most food survive and generate offspring for the next round. The number of offspring is proportional to the food collected, and each offspring may undergo additional mutations. Slimes can survive up to 10 seconds without food, and consuming food resets their survival timer back to 10 seconds.

Victory Conditions

  • Each round lasts 1 minute. After a species survives for the full duration of a round, it counts as a successful round. Once a species survives three rounds, the simulation ends and that species is confirmed as successful.
